Shaded Maps

a gallery of detailed shaded maps, digitally manufactured with Python, PDAL and GDAL tools from elevation, point cloud and OpenStreetMap data

The Shaded Maps project, born by chance during the COVID pandemic on an island in the Mediterranean Sea, is the brainchild of a research software engineer with a passion for maps. This project involves creating a collection of images that represent shadows on each pixel of a map, caused by obstructions between the pixel and various positions of the sun in the sky.


Obstructions are depicted using high-resolution digital surface models, which are sometimes already available through national, regional, or city geoportals, or otherwise generated from open data LiDAR point clouds.


Creating a single shaded map requires:

  • an extensive and detailed digital surface model;
  • the identification of areas covered by water (e.g., ocean, sea, lakes, rivers, canals);
  • the computationally expensive calculation of shadows for each pixel and for 250+ sun positions;
  • the transformation of the calculated shadows into images.

Morever, many other difficulties challenges are tackled:

  • finding the necessary data, which is often difficult as it may not exist, or the geoportal is complicated to navigate by design or because of language barrier;
  • ensuring the data is of high quality (resolution should not exceed one meter) and available for a sufficiently large area (at least 10 km x 10 km);
  • building the digital surface model from raster files, if available, or after the processing of LiDAR point cloud files;
  • archiving and managing 15+ TB of raw data, processed data, metadata and images;
  • publishing the shaded maps on the web, which is not so straightforward when the images are huge.
